Gradient

Elements Explaination Example
Linear Gradient
General Defined by its direction or angle (Stops: at least two)
Using Direction linear-gradient(to side/corner, color-stop1, color-stop2, ...)
(Default direction: to bottom)
(Stops: at least two)
linear-gradient(green, yellow);
linear-gradient(to right, green, yellow)
linear-gradient(to bottom right, green, yellow)
linear-gradient(green, yellow, orange)
Using Angles linear-gradient(angle, color-stop1, color-stop2, ...)
(Default angle: 180deg -> to bottom)
(0deg: to top | 90deg: to right | 180deg: to bottom | 270deg: to left)
(Stops: at least two)
linear-gradient(180deg, green, yellow);
linear-gradient(90deg, green, yellow)
linear-gradient(270deg, green, yellow)
linear-gradient(green, yellow, orange)
Using Transparency Using RGBA color as stops
linear-gradient(rgba(255,0,0,0), rgba(255,0,0,1))
Repeating repeating-linear-gradient()
repeating-linear-gradient(red, yellow 10%, green 20%)
Radial Gradient
General Defined by its center. (Stops: at least two color stops)
Syntax radial-gradient(shape size at position, start-color, ..., last-color)
Default: evenly spaced color stops
radial-gradient(red, yellow, green)
Differently spaced color stops
radial-gradient(red 5%, yellow 15%, green 60%)
shape: ellipse (default) or circle
radial-gradient(circle, red, yellow, green)
size: closest-side | farthest-side | closest-corner | farthest-corner (default)
radial-gradient(closest-side at 10% 50%, red, yellow, black)
position: 50% 50% - center (default)
radial-gradient(closest-side at 50% 50%, red, yellow, black)
radial-gradient(closest-side at 70% 50%, red, yellow, black)
Repeating repeating-radial-gradient()
repeating-radial-gradient(red, yellow 10%, green 15%)
